History & Legends

Langkawi’s recorded story stretches back more than a millennium, but its most famous legend is that of Mahsuri, a lovely maiden wrongly accused of adultery in the late 18th century. With her dying breath she laid a seven‑generation curse on the island; locals say it finally lifted in the 1980s—just before tourism began to flourish. Today that tale lives on at Makam Mahsuri, while prehistoric traces can be explored inside the UNESCO Global Geopark that preserves 550‑million‑year‑old limestone formations.

Culture & Traditions

  • Island Hospitality. Locals greet visitors with the warm “Hai! Selamat Datang.”
  • Traditional Rice Farming. Watch water buffalo plough terraces in Laman Padi and learn how beras shaped village life.
  • Arts & Crafts. Hand‑dyed batik, intricate songket weaving, and coconut‑shell carving make perfect souvenirs.
  • Festivals. From vibrant Hari Raya Aidilfitri open houses to the Langkawi International Maritime & Aerospace (LIMA) show, the calendar is lively year‑round.
  • Cuisine. Don’t leave without tasting laksa ikan sekoq, charcoal‑grilled ikan bakar, and sea‑salted coconut ice cream.
